💰 Return on Equity (ROE) Calculation

Return on Equity (ROE) is a measure of financial performance calculated by dividing net income by shareholders' equity.

$$ \text{ROE} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Shareholders' Equity}} \times 100\% $$

This quarter's ROE is **14.5%**, reflecting strong profitability relative to equity financing.

Our ROE of 14.5% is an increase over last quarter, demonstrating effective utilization of shareholder capital. We'll be using this formula to analyze divisional performance next.

🐍 Python Risk Scoring Service

Example snippet from our new risk assessment microservice.


                    def calculate_risk_score(transaction_amount, client_history):
                        # Base score is calculated from transaction amount
                        base_score = transaction_amount * 0.05
                        
                        # Apply historical risk multiplier
                        history_multiplier = 1.0 - (client_history['fraud_count'] * 0.1)
                        
                        # Final score
                        return max(0, base_score * history_multiplier)
This function is the core of our real-time risk engine. Line 3 shows the base risk calculation, while line 6 incorporates a penalty based on the client's historical fraud count, allowing us to dynamically adjust risk assessment.
